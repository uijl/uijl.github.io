Reading

Below is a list of the books I have been reading, it is not as long as I would like it to be.

2020

  • Thinking, Fast and Slow - Daniel Kahneman Very insightful.
  • The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People - Stephen R. Covey

2019

  • The Intelligent Investor - Benjamin Graham A great introduction into value investing. And a conclusion if you do not have the time to pick value stocks: invest in trackers and don’t chicken out if there is a crash.
  • “Surely You’re Joking, Mr. Feynman!” - Richard P. Feynman This book is amazing. Feyhnman takes you on a journey through his exceptional life.
  • Rich Dad, Poor Dad - Robert Kiyosaki On his podcast the author seems to be very arrogant, the book is similar. But there is some truth in what he is saying.
  • The Richest man in Babylon - George Clason Both this book and Rich Dad, Poor Dad get recommended often if you want to learn more about becom financially independent, but I do not find it that insightfull. It basically comes down to three things: Do not spend more than you earn / have, Save at least 10% of income, Make your money work for you.
  • The Black Swan - Nassim Nicholas Taleb I enjoy the insights and the way of thinking that Taleb presents.

2018

  • Sapiens - Yuval Noah Hariri I read this Book, just like everyone else. But unlike most people, I found it kind of hard to get through.
  • Moneyball - Michael Lewis I like the books by Michael Lewis, I have read two books by him this year.
  • Born a Crime - Trevor Noah Loved it.
  • Liar’s Poker - Michael Lewis The second book of Michael Lewis that I read in 2018.
  • The Sellout - Paul Beatty I do not think that I understood it. It was very fun, and I love satire, but I feel like I missed something.
  • Against the Gods - Peter Bernstein Interesting book on the definiting of risk and probability throughout the modern times.
  • Origin - Dan Brown The only true novel I read this year. I always like to books by Dan Brown, with recurring patterns of symbolics and art history.
